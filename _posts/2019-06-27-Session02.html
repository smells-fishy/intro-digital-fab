Class 2 (June 27)

During Thursday's lecture, we explored the functions of voltmeters and how to use the Arduino board as a voltmeter. First, we used voltmeters to measure the voltage across the circuits we built during Wednesday's lab session. We used voltmeters with bare wire leads to allow for ease of attachment to the circuit. Notice that the voltmeter is attached to the circuit in parallel, and that the voltage was measured directly across the LED. This setup yields the forward voltage drop of the LED. However, it must be noted that voltage drops measured from the LED cannot be used for circuit analysis via Ohm's Law, as LEDs do not obey Ohm's Law (LEDs have an exponential relationship between voltage and current). If analysis of the circuit is desired, then the voltage drop must be measured across the resistor in the circuit. Next, we examined the usage of the Arduino board as a voltmeter. Using the analogRead() command, Arduino can read voltages through the "analog" pins on the board. All that is required is to change the circuit such that the ground wire goes into any one of the analog input slots. As you can see, the program reads the signal from the Arduino analog pin and then displays it onto the console using the Serial.begin() and Serial.prinln() commands. The signal value can be converted into voltage by multiplying by 5 (5V is the maximum) and dividing by 1023 (1024-1, the maximum values the analog pin can read).
